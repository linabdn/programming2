The Château de Vitry-sur-Seine was built in 1710 by Claude-François Paparel, Treasurer of the Wars and Gendarmerie for Louis XIV. It was originally constructed as a seigneurial estate, with Paparel holding various feudal rights over the local inhabitants. Indeed, the castle served as the seat of power of for the area. Paparel was adsministrating the land and collecting taxes. However, he was arrested and his assets, including the castle were seized in 1716 dut to financial mismanagement



Front view of the castle from July 1901

The estate was then owned by different people over the years. In 1719, the Marquis de la Fare, Captain of the Guards of the Regent, acquired the lordship. Later, the Marshal d'Alègre took ownership and used the château as storage for grain and fodder. In the 18th and 19th centuries, the château passed through various owners.

In the early 20th century, the castle's estate was sold in parcels by the heirs for urban development. The main castle, known as the "grand château" was demolished in 1910 for housing projects. The small castle survived until 1930 when it was demolished to construct public baths.

Through the work of Camil Kermoume, the exact location of the château was pinpointed. The majority of the château’s footprint is now situated in the current playground of the Danielle-Casanova school, near the Church of Saint-Germain. Very little remains of the castle.
